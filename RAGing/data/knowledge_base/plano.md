# City of Plano Rental Codes and Tenant Rights (Collin County)

## 1. Heating and Cooling Standards
- **General Rules**: The City of Plano adopts the International Property Maintenance Code (IPMC) for building and mechanical systems.
- **Heating Requirement**: Landlords must provide heating facilities capable of maintaining a temperature of 68°F (20°C) in all habitable rooms, bathrooms, and toilet rooms.
- **Cooling Requirement**: There is no specific municipal ordinance mandating the installation of air conditioning. However, if air conditioning was provided at the start of the lease, the landlord is required to maintain it in working order. Under Texas Property Code § 92.052, a breakdown in cooling that threatens health or safety in extreme heat must be repaired within a reasonable timeframe (presumed 7 days).
- **Code Violation Reports**: Tenants can contact the Plano Property Standards Division to inspect code violations.

## 2. Multi-Family Rental Registration
- **Mandatory Registry**: Apartment complexes in Plano with 5 or more units that are 5 years old or older must register annually with the City of Plano.
- **Inspections**: Registered complexes are subject to periodic habitability and safety inspections by Plano Code Enforcement.
- **Single-Family Rentals**: Plano does not require registration for standard long-term single-family residential rental properties.

## 3. Short-Term Rental (STR) Registration
- **Mandatory Permit**: All short-term rentals (rented for less than 30 consecutive days) must obtain an annual permit and comply with city occupancy and safety codes.

## 4. Local Legal and Advocacy Resources (Plano/Collin County)
- **Legal Aid of Northwest Texas (LANWT) McKinney Office**: Serves Collin County residents with free legal support. Phone: (972) 542-9405.
- **Plano Housing Authority**: Provides housing choice vouchers and community resource referrals. Phone: (972) 420-3530.
