# City of Austin Rental Codes and Tenant Rights (Travis County)

## 1. Cooling and Heating Standards
- **Cooling Requirements**: Under Austin Property Maintenance Code Section 302.13, landlords are required to provide air conditioning equipment for all rental units. The equipment must be capable of cooling the interior of all habitable rooms to either:
  - 85°F (29.4°C) or lower, or
  - At least 15°F (-9.4°C) cooler than the outdoor temperature, whichever temperature is warmer.
- **Heating Requirements**: Heating equipment must be capable of maintaining a temperature of at least 68°F (20°C) in all habitable rooms when the outdoor temperature falls below freezing.
- **Enforcement**: Tenants can report heating and cooling failures to Austin 311. Code Enforcement officers can inspect the property and fine landlords up to $1,000 per day for non-compliance.

## 2. Tenant Relocation Assistance Ordinance (Austin City Code Chapter 25-1, Article 11)
- **Relocation Benefits**: If a developer or landlord intends to demolish, rezone, or substantially rehabilitate a multi-family complex (resulting in the displacement of tenants), they must provide relocation assistance.
- **Eligibility**: Low-income tenants (earning 80% or less of the Area Median Family Income) are entitled to relocation payments.
- **Notice Period**: Landlords must notify tenants at least 120 days before requesting a demolition permit or displacement. Relocation payments include moving expenses and rent assistance, paid by the developer or landlord.

## 3. Austin Repeat Offender Program (ROP)
- **What it is**: The Repeat Offender Program (ROP) is a rental registration program designed to identify rental properties that consistently violate building and health codes.
- **Criteria**: A property is placed on the ROP register if it receives multiple citations for health, safety, or structural violations within a 12-month period, or fails to comply with Code Enforcement repair orders.
- **Impact**: Registered properties are subjected to mandatory periodic inspections (at least once per year) and must pay higher registration fees.

## 4. Local Legal and Advocacy Resources (Austin/Travis County)
- **Austin Tenants Council (ATC)**: Provides counseling, mediation, and advocacy services for renters. Website: www.housing-rights.org. Phone: (512) 474-1961.
- **Travis County Law Library**: Provides legal forms and self-help guides for eviction defense.
- **Texas RioGrande Legal Aid (TRLA)**: Provides free legal representation to low-income tenants in eviction suits. Phone: 1-888-988-9996.
