# City of Frisco Rental Codes and Tenant Rights (Collin & Denton Counties)

## 1. Heating and Cooling Standards
- **IPMC Adoption**: Frisco adopts the 2024 International Property Maintenance Code (IPMC) which outlines safety and structural integrity standards.
- **Heating Requirement**: Heating facilities must be maintained in good working condition. If heat is not supplied, it is considered a major code violation during winter months.
- **Air Conditioning**: There is no specific municipal mandate requiring landlords to install AC in long-term rentals. However, if AC is provided in the lease agreement or existed at move-in, it must be maintained. If it fails, the tenant must submit a written notice. If the landlord fails to repair it, the tenant can report the violation to Frisco Code Enforcement.

## 2. Rental Property Registration
- **Short-Term Rentals (STRs)**: Frisco has a strict registration ordinance for properties rented for less than 30 consecutive days. STR operators must register annually, pay a $300 fee, and prove compliance with fire safety codes.
- **Long-Term Rentals**: The city does not require registration or licensing for standard, long-term single-family rental properties.

## 3. Local Legal and Advocacy Resources (Frisco/Collin & Denton)
- **Legal Aid of Northwest Texas (LANWT) Denton Office**: Serves Denton County residents. Phone: (940) 387-5357.
- **Denton County Housing Authority**: Rental housing and utility assistance referrals. Phone: (940) 383-1504.
- **Frisco Development Services (Code Enforcement)**: Reports of structural safety, utility failures, or minimum housing standard violations. Phone: (972) 292-5300.
