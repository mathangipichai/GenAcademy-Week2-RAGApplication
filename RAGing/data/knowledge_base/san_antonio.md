# City of San Antonio Rental Codes and Tenant Rights (Bexar County)

## 1. Cooling and Heating Standards
- **Cooling Requirements**: Under the San Antonio Property Maintenance Code, landlords are required to maintain cooling systems in all rental units. The cooling system must be capable of maintaining a temperature of:
  - 80°F (26.7°C) or cooler in all habitable rooms, or
  - At least 15°F (-9.4°C) cooler than the outdoor temperature, whichever is warmer.
- **Heating Requirements**: Heating facilities must be capable of maintaining a temperature of 68°F (20°C) in all habitable rooms when the outdoor temperature is below freezing.
- **Reporting**: Tenants can report heating and cooling failures to San Antonio Code Enforcement by calling 311 or (210) 207-6000.

## 2. San Antonio Notice of Tenant Rights Ordinance
- **Tenant Bill of Rights**: San Antonio requires all landlords of residential rental properties to provide a "Notice of Tenant Rights and Resources" document to a tenant:
  - At the time of lease signing, and
  - Along with any Notice to Vacate for non-payment of rent.
- **Fines**: A landlord who fails to provide this notice can be fined up to $500 per violation.
- **Content**: The notice outlines the renter's right to request repairs, the eviction process, and a list of local rental assistance programs.

## 3. Eviction Court (Bexar County)
- In Bexar County, eviction lawsuits are filed in one of the four Justice of the Peace courts. If a landlord gives a 3-day Notice to Vacate, the tenant does not have to move out immediately; the landlord must file a lawsuit, and only a JP judge can order an eviction.

## 4. Local Legal and Advocacy Resources (San Antonio/Bexar County)
- **Texas RioGrande Legal Aid (TRLA)**: Headquarters in San Antonio. Provides free eviction defense and tenant advocacy. Website: www.trla.org. Phone: (210) 212-3700.
- **City of San Antonio Fair Housing Division**: Investigates housing discrimination and landlord-tenant disputes. Phone: (210) 207-5910.
- **San Antonio Tenant Union**: A community organization fighting for affordable housing and renter rights.
