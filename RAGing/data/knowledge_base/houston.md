# City of Houston Rental Codes and Tenant Rights (Harris County)

## 1. Space Heating and Cooling Standards
- **Cooling Requirements**: Under Houston Code of Ordinances Chapter 10, Article IX, Division 3, Section 10-363, landlords must provide and maintain space cooling systems. The system must be capable of cooling all habitable rooms to:
  - 78°F (25.6°C), or
  - 20°F (11.1°C) below the outside temperature, whichever is warmer.
- **Heating Requirements**: Landlords must provide heating systems capable of warming all habitable rooms to 70°F (21.1°C) when the outdoor temperature is 20°F or higher.
- **Enforcement**: Complaints are handled by Houston 311 or Houston Health and Human Services.

## 2. Multi-Family Habitability and Mold Rules
- **Structural Integrity**: The landlord must maintain roofs, exterior walls, and windows to be weather-tight. Standing water or structural leaks that cause mold growth are violations of Houston's Minimum Housing Standards.
- **Leaking Pipes**: Houston ordinances require landlords to fix plumbing leaks within 72 hours of receiving written notice from the tenant if the leak threatens the structural integrity or causes standing water/sewage backup.

## 3. Towing Regulations from Apartment Complexes (Houston Chapter 26)
- **Required Signage**: Under Houston City Code Chapter 26, landlords cannot tow a tenant's or guest's vehicle from an apartment parking lot unless there are signs at all entrances stating "Unauthorized Vehicles Will Be Towed" with specific size, font, and contact information for the vehicle storage facility.
- **Lease Provision**: The parking rules and towing policy must be explicitly stated in the lease agreement signed by the tenant.

## 4. Local Legal and Advocacy Resources (Houston/Harris County)
- **Lone Star Legal Aid (LSLA)**: Provides free civil legal services to low-income Texans in the Houston area. Website: www.lonestarlegal.org. Phone: (713) 652-0077.
- **Houston Tenants Union**: A grassroots group that organizes tenants to fight landlord abuse, evictions, and neglect.
- **Harris County Justice Courts**: The legal surface where landlords file evictions. The Harris County Law Library provides self-help eviction packets.
